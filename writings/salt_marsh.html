I will choose to drown this year in the salt marsh;
the sweet, clear streams I won't force myself upon,
won't dirty with this windowless pathos.
we can choose, always, the bodies we won't take to,
the alms we will refuse, no matter how hungry we are,
no matter how tasteful the praise may be.

not in classrooms but continuity, will I walk the floors of the seas,
never to be found by those loving lifeboats.
