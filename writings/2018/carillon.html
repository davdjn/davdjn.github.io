now that we're older,
moments of silence have become
too far and too easy for us to bear,
so sometimes we need reminding

this is not for their sake, but for ours

in winding belfries and weeping towers,
they sing painful and deafening-
clarity is born past the point of desperation,
sobriety cuts beyond calm and calamity

I swear we will make up our losses

life becomes
uninterrupted
without tragedies to remind us
how unnatural
calm is

our stillness is the deepest of puddles

If this is death, I need you
to be here and see this
If this means anything to you, I needed you
to be here and hear this



sometimes, we forget about that terrible
transcendence
found only in the
tallest of
towers
